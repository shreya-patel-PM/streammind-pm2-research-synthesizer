INTERVIEW TRANSCRIPT — USER RESEARCH
Topic: Subscription downgrade flow experience
Date: May 14, 2026
Interviewer: Sam (Researcher)
Participant: Marcus, 28, Software Engineer, Austin
Duration: 19 minutes

---

SAM: Thanks for taking time, Marcus. I want to understand the downgrade experience you had. Where do you want to start?

MARCUS: I want to start with the fact that I almost canceled instead of downgrading. That's the headline.

SAM: Tell me what was going on.

MARCUS: So I'd been on the Premium tier for maybe a year. I share the account with my girlfriend, and her brother, and my mom. Four people, four streams, made sense. Then my girlfriend moved out — different story, don't worry about it — and suddenly I was paying eighteen bucks a month for a tier built around four-person households.

SAM: Got it. So a life change reduced what you actually needed.

MARCUS: Yeah. And I'll be honest, my first instinct wasn't downgrade. My first instinct was cancel. Because the service felt tied to that period of my life and I was kind of done with it.

SAM: What changed your mind?

MARCUS: I tried to cancel and the flow was so aggressive I got annoyed and just closed the tab. So they kept charging me for another month because I never finished the cancellation. Which, honestly, made me angrier.

SAM: Talk to me about what was aggressive about it.

MARCUS: Okay, so. I click "Cancel subscription." It asks me why. Fine, I pick a reason. It then shows me a screen that's like, "Did you know about all these features you're losing?" with like seven bullet points. I click continue. It shows me an offer — three months at half price. I click "No thanks." It shows me another offer — first month free. I click "No thanks." It shows me ANOTHER offer — a downgrade to a cheaper tier instead. And I'm like, why is this the third option? This should have been the first option.

SAM: That's interesting. So the downgrade path was hidden behind the cancellation path.

MARCUS: Exactly. To find the downgrade I had to go through the cancellation flow far enough that they were offering it as a last-ditch attempt. That's bad design. Or it's good design if your goal is to confuse people, but I don't think it should be.

SAM: When you saw the downgrade option, did you take it?

MARCUS: Yeah, eventually. I came back the next day after I'd cooled off. But I want to be clear — if I hadn't come back, they would have lost me entirely. Because the cancellation flow was so frustrating I just closed the tab the first time. I didn't downgrade, I didn't cancel, I just left. And the next month they charged me again, and I was even more annoyed.

SAM: What about the downgrade itself once you went through with it? How was that experience?

MARCUS: Anticlimactic, which is the right answer. Once I found it, it was two clicks. Confirm the new tier, confirm the new price. Done. Which made the previous experience even more annoying in retrospect, because the actual mechanics were simple. It was just buried.

SAM: Did they offer you a retention discount during the downgrade itself?

MARCUS: They did, kind of. They offered me a deal to stay on the Premium tier at a discount. Which I said no to. And then it was like, okay, downgrade complete, see you in a month. Which felt fine. The retention offer didn't bother me, it was just the fifteen previous things they'd tried that bothered me.

SAM: Were any of the retention offers tempting?

MARCUS: No. And here's the thing — none of them addressed why I was leaving. I wasn't leaving because Premium was too expensive. I was leaving because I no longer needed Premium. Those are different problems. A discount on Premium doesn't help me. A different tier that fits my new situation does. So when they finally surfaced the downgrade option, I took it immediately. But getting there was a maze.

SAM: How do you feel about the service now that you're on a cheaper tier?

MARCUS: Better, actually. Which is interesting. I think because the relationship feels more right-sized. I'm not paying for things I don't use. I'm not feeling resentful every time the charge hits my card. I watch maybe three hours a week, and now the price reflects that.

SAM: Will you recommend the service to friends?

MARCUS: Mmm. Probably? With caveats. I'd tell them to make sure they're on the right tier from the start and not to assume they need Premium. And I'd warn them about the cancellation flow. Like — heads up, if you ever want to leave, prepare for a fight.

SAM: That's a really sharp observation. One last thing — if you could change one thing about the downgrade experience, what would it be?

MARCUS: Surface the downgrade option BEFORE the cancellation flow. Just have a "Change plan" option that's as prominent as "Cancel subscription." Don't make me go through cancellation theater to find the option I actually wanted. That's the whole thing. Don't make me jump through hoops to give you money.

SAM: Thanks, Marcus. This was really useful.

MARCUS: Yeah, no problem.

[END OF TRANSCRIPT]
