INTERVIEW TRANSCRIPT — USER RESEARCH
Topic: Subscription downgrade flow experience
Date: May 16, 2026
Interviewer: Sam (Researcher)
Participant: Diane, 56, Retired Teacher, Phoenix
Duration: 24 minutes

---

SAM: Diane, thanks for joining. I'd love to hear about the experience you had recently with downgrading your subscription. Where would you like to start?

DIANE: Well, let me start by saying I didn't know I was on the wrong tier. That was the whole thing. My daughter set up the account for me maybe three years ago when I retired and moved. She picked the Premium tier because she said it was the best one. And I went along with that. And it wasn't until recently that I realized I literally didn't know I was paying for a tier that included things I never used.

SAM: How did you discover that?

DIANE: My granddaughter, of all people. She was over for dinner and we were watching a show together and I said something about the picture being clear, and she said, "Oh Grandma, you're on Premium? You don't even have a 4K TV." And then she explained what the different tiers were. And I was a little embarrassed honestly.

SAM: That's not embarrassing — I think that's a pretty common situation. So what did you do after that conversation?

DIANE: I sat down at my computer the next morning and I tried to figure out how to change my plan. And it took me a while. I'm not the most technically savvy. I found the cancel option first, because that was clearly labeled. But I didn't want to cancel. I wanted to keep watching, I just wanted a different plan.

SAM: How did you eventually find the option to change?

DIANE: I had to Google it. I literally searched for "how to change subscription tier" and that brought me to a help article that had instructions. Once I followed the instructions, it was fine. But I shouldn't have had to Google it. It should have been right there with the cancel option.

SAM: When you went through the downgrade itself, how did that feel?

DIANE: It was strange. They kept asking me if I was sure. And they showed me things I'd lose, but a lot of those things I didn't even know I had. So it was a weird feeling. Like — "you'll lose simultaneous streaming on four devices." I never streamed on four devices. I only have one TV. I never even knew that was a feature.

SAM: So the warnings about lost features didn't feel relevant to you.

DIANE: Not at all. They felt like they were trying to scare me into staying on Premium. But what they were warning me about losing were things I wasn't using. So the warnings sort of confirmed I was on the wrong tier. They were doing the opposite of what they intended, in my case.

SAM: That's a really interesting point. Did they offer you any kind of discount or incentive to stay?

DIANE: They did. They offered me a discount — I think 50% off for three months. And I almost took it. Because honestly the discount was very generous and I thought, well, that's a good deal. But then I thought about it more and I realized — I'd still be paying for things I don't use, just at a discount. And in three months it would go back to full price and I'd be in the same situation. So I said no.

SAM: That's a really considered decision. Walk me through how you felt about the retention offer overall.

DIANE: I appreciated that they offered. I think because I was an older customer who'd been with them for a while, they wanted to acknowledge that. And I felt acknowledged. So I would say the retention offer was the best part of the experience. At least they tried to keep me. Even though I didn't take it.

SAM: How are you feeling about the service now that you've downgraded?

DIANE: I'm happy. I'm paying about half of what I was paying. The picture quality is fine on my old TV — I genuinely cannot tell the difference. There are some ads now, but they're short. I watch maybe two hours a day, and now it costs me what feels like a reasonable amount. I should have done this two years ago.

SAM: One thing I want to ask — what could the service have done to help you avoid being on the wrong tier for so long?

DIANE: Oh, that's a good question. I think — maybe once a year, they could send me an email that says, "Hi Diane, based on your usage, you might be on the wrong tier. Here are tiers that might fit better." That would have been so helpful. Because I was paying them more than I needed to for years and they had all the data to know that. They could have told me. They didn't.

SAM: That's a really powerful observation. The data was there and the recommendation wasn't.

DIANE: Right. And I think there's something almost — I don't want to say dishonest, but it's not honest either. They knew I wasn't using four streams. They knew I wasn't watching in 4K. They had all that information. And they just kept charging me Premium prices anyway. I'm not mad about it. I should have paid more attention. But it would have been nice if they'd made it easier.

SAM: Will you recommend the service to friends?

DIANE: Yes, but with a tip. I'll tell them to make sure they're on the right tier and to check every year or so. I lost probably four hundred dollars over two years because I was on a tier I didn't need. Not their fault, but also they could have helped.

SAM: Last question. If you had to describe the whole downgrade experience in one phrase, what would it be?

DIANE: Harder than it needed to be, with a soft landing at the end.

SAM: That's a great line. Thanks, Diane.

DIANE: You're welcome, dear.

[END OF TRANSCRIPT]
